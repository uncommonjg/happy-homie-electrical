# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Williamson County, TN homeowners — skewing 35–65, high income, house-proud. They're on their phone when something breaks or when they're improving the house. They've been burned before: contractor didn't show, didn't call back, left a mess. They're not price-shopping — they're trust-shopping. They want to hire someone and not think about it again.

## Product Purpose

Convert homeowners into booked service calls. The site is a trust-builder first, lead-gen tool second. Success = homeowner feels confident enough to tap "Book Online" or call. Secondary purpose: establish Happy Homie as the premium residential electrician brand in Williamson County before the market figures out they exist.

## Brand Personality

Neighborly, sharp, accountable. The electrician you'd actually want to grab a coffee with. Not corporate — not bro-ey. Professional without being stiff. Family-owned and proud of it, not in a sentimental way, in a "we live here too" way.

## Anti-references

- HomeAdvisor / Thumbtack / Angie's List — generic, commoditized, zero brand
- Large regional electrical contractor sites (Mr. Electric, Mister Sparky) — corporate, generic, no personality
- Clip-art lightning bolt logos and stock photo truck photos
- Anything that looks like it was built in 2012 from a trades template

## Design Principles

1. **Trust is the product.** Every element — photo, copy, pricing callout, face of Joel and Alex — builds or erodes trust. Nothing decorative that isn't also trustworthy.
2. **Phone-first, always.** The homeowner is on the couch or in the kitchen with a broken outlet. Design every interaction for a thumb on a 390px screen first.
3. **Premium but human.** Williamson County can afford premium. The brand earns a high rate with professionalism, not by being slick or cold. The warmth has to come through.
4. **No hedging.** The site says what it does clearly and asks for the booking. No vague "contact us for a quote." Flat rates, real faces, real service areas, real CTA.
5. **Family-owned is the differentiator.** This isn't a tagline — it's the reason the business exists. Every dollar stays local. That's the pitch against both the big contractors and the unreliable solos.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

- WCAG AA minimum. Body text ≥ 4.5:1 contrast on all backgrounds.
- Touch targets ≥ 44px on all interactive elements — the primary user context is a phone.
- Reduced motion: fade-up animations should respect `prefers-reduced-motion`.
- Forms must be keyboard navigable with visible focus states.
